Get to know me Professionally

I graduated BS Nursing back in 2011 and passed the boards the same year. Unfortunately I did not practice my profession, instead I first worked as a citiphone officer in Citibank in early 2012 until June 2013 and in my second company WNS from 2013 to 2015. I had good four years of experience in the BPO industry.

In 2016, I decided to pursue my dream to become a chef, so I studied culinary. I graduated after a year and did my intership in Jakarta, Indonesia for 6 months. Right after, I landed my first job as a private chef for six months. Then, I got my first restaurant job as a Sous chef for Dr Wine which I was part of the pioneer team back in 2017 and I did one good year with them. Then, I got my first big hotel job as a chef de partie in Crimson Hotel Alabang to which I laste six months. I moved to Peninsula Hotel afterwards, also as a chef de partiie until covid hit back in 2020. Big hotels closed down, but luckily, I landed a Head chef position in a small boutique hotel in Makati which turned out to be my last step in the kitchen.

In early 2021 I decided to take another path, so I studied online courses about Digital Marketing and luckily got my first job as a VA for an Australian Real estate company. And then my life changed since then. I am still working as a VA until now and I words can't express how grateful I am for this opportunity. I was able to work from home and from anywhere I wanted. Because of this, I stumbled across different opportunities one being a developer. I got interested in coding hence I decided to pursue this path.

I might be called "jack of all trade" kind of person, but for me it is better to know a lot of things than being stuck with only one skill. It can be beneificial in the future and I enjoy learning new things!

Top 3 Travel Destination

Bali, Indonesia

Bali, Indonesia has been and will always be my favorite travel destination. Despite being there few times, there is still this feeling that this place is always inviting me to come. Bali is where I celebrated my 30th birthday and since then I vowed to myself that every year I will come back. I am even considering living there for few months. Bali is magnificent. There's so much things to do, from hiking to surfing to beach bumming, bar hopping and food crawl to name a few.

Bohol, Philippines

Bohol, Philippines would be my second. I lived on the island of Panglao for almost a year during the height of pandemic. I have met a lot of good people and being sorrounded by nature definitely gave a really good feeling. Like Bali, there are a lot of things you can do in Bohol. From hiking, diving, island hopping, river tours, kayaking, paddle boarding and a lot more.

Paris, France

Despite not being able to visit this country, Paris France will be my third favorite. There were so many things you can also do in France based on my research plus french food is amazing! Hopefully by next year I will be able to make it there. I would love to see the Eiffel tower, The Louvre Mueseum and go hiking skii in the alps.
